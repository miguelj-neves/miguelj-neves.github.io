About Me

I am a PhD candidate of geophysics at the Georgia Institute of Technology where I study Seismology. My work focuses on earthquake detection to swiftly analyze years of data and improve earthquake catalogs to get a better understanding of earthquake physics. I use this new data to gain insights on earthquake nucleation, aftershock propagation and earthquake interactions in both interplate and intraplate regions. I am also interested in “unusual” signals such as tremor and what they can reveal about “regular” earthquakes.
